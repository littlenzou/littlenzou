About Little Nzou

The Little Elephant
Who Remembers

In Shona, nzou means elephant, the animal that never forgets, that carries its heritage across vast distances. Little Nzou was born in Zimbabwe, raised on stories of the hare and the baboon, and arrived in Dubai with a Nikon D3400 camera, a design eye, and something to prove.

"Great creativity doesn't shout, it resonates. Every frame, every logo, every motion sequence should carry a feeling the audience didn't expect but immediately recognises as true."

How We Work

From Brief to Brilliance

Every Little Nzou project follows a clear, collaborative process — so you always know where we are, what comes next, and what to expect at every stage.

01

Discovery & Brief

We start with a detailed consultation — exploring your goals, audience, competitors, and vision. A creative brief is drafted and agreed before any work begins.

Day 1 — 2

02

Moodboard & Direction

We present two to three distinct visual directions — each with a moodboard, colour palette, and tone of voice reference. You choose the path forward.

Day 3 — 5

03

Design & Production

The studio builds. Whether it is a logo suite, motion sequence, or video edit — work is developed with precision and regular progress updates shared throughout.

Day 6 — 14

04

Review & Refinement

You receive the first presentation with our rationale. Feedback is gathered and up to two rounds of revision are incorporated — structured, not open-ended.

Day 15 — 18

05

Final Approval

Once the work is approved, final files are prepared across all required formats — print, digital, social, and broadcast as applicable to your project.

Day 19 — 20

06

Delivery & Handover

A structured deliverable pack is handed over — organised files, usage guidelines, and a short walkthrough so you can hit the ground running from day one.

Day 21

07

Post-Launch Support

The relationship does not end at delivery. A two-week support window ensures any implementation questions or minor adjustments are handled without delay.

Day 22 — 35
